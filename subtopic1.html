Genetic Engineering

URL Genome.gov
Author National Human Genome Research Institute
Abstract This site allows you to have a brief understanding of genetic engineering. Genetic engineering is a process that uses technologies to change or alter the DNA makeup of an organism. The process requires changing a single base pair, adding a new part of DNA, or deleting a part of DNA. Genetic engineering has been applied to many industries, such as the production of cancer therapies, genetically modifying plants, brewing yeasts, and livestock. The main idea is to add or to "delete" a gene to produce a desired organism. It has been used for cloning to being used for understanding new biomedical discoveries. It is a fascinating subject to study once you truly understand the cause.

Abstract This site allows you to have a full understanding of genetic engineering. It is the manipulation, modification, etc of DNA with the purpose of modifying the organism or organisms to a desirable state. Genetic engineering refers to the methods of recombinant DNA technology. Through genetic engineering, medical products such as human insulin, human growth hormone, and hepatitis B vaccine derived from this engineering. The possibility of Recombinant DNA technology emerged in 1968 by Swiss microbiologist Werner Arber. It was later pioneered in 1973 by American biochemists who are among the first to cut DNA into fragments. Most of these procedures require the insertion of foreign genes in plasmids.
