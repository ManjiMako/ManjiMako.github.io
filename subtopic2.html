Abstract This article talks about the definition of bioinformatics and an overview from David Adams who is a senior clinician. Bioinformatics is related to genetics and genomics. Bioinformatics uses computers to collect, store, and analyze biological data and information. DNA and amino acids are the types of data that is collected and analyzed. Bioinformatics is important because scientists, doctors, clinicians use them to increase the understanding of health and diseases. Before bioinformatics, the issue was getting to the information. Dr. David Adams says that with this challenge being solved, the new challenge is trying to understand and interpreting the information that was collected.
