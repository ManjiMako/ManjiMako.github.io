Author National Library of Medicine
Abstract This article is on the relevance of the history of biotechnology for healthcare. The definition of biotechnology is a multidisciplinary science that uses biological systems to address scientific and medical challenges. Biotechnology surprisingly is not a new innovation but something that dates back to ancient times. Due to no scientific knowledge, humans relied on the experience of breeding plant and animal species and fermentation. In the past four decades, biotechnology has evolved into a discipline that utilizes molecular biology, genetic engineering, etc to produce new products. This method has paved the way for treatments and technologies that were previously deemed impossible.

Abstract This article explains biotechnology in medicine. Biotechnology is important because knowledge of genetic makeup of our species and genetic technology to manipulate and fix genes that were mutated helps to provide methods to treat diseases. This article also mentions pharmacogenomics, which is the study of how genetic inheritance affects one’s response to drugs. The benefits are development of tailor made medicines, more accurate methods of giving the right dosage of drugs, and better and safe vaccines. Another way biotechnology is used is for genetic diagnosis and gene therapy. Genetic testing is the direct examination of the DNA molecule that is then taken for two major types of gene testing.
